Historical Places in Gauteng

Mandela House

Once a family residence of South Africa's legendary president, Mandela House features some interactive exhibits and personal objects. Although small, the house provides an important glimpse into Mandela's private life.

Union Building

Union Buildings the official seat of the South African government that overlooks the city. The neo-classical buildings were built using local sandstonemore than a century ago, and the iconic twin towers are said to represent the Afrikaans and English languages. You won't be able to tour the buildings themselves, but you can stroll through the terraced gardens surrounding them for views of the city below. The gardens feature numerous monuments honoring influential South African figures, including a towering bronze statue of Nelson Mandela.

Melrose House

Melrose House is a historic-house museum built in 1886 by a local businessman. The three-story mansion features turrets and Dutch gables, with colorful stained glass windows and ornate ceilings and fireplaces inside. Requisitioned during the Second Boer War, the house served as the military headquarters for the British imperial troops. A period room in which the Treaty of Vereeniging was signed is carefully preserved.
